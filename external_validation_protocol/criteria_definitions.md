# Criteria Definitions for Expert Review

Use these definitions when reviewing ISMS-Bench Incident Response cases.

## Core Incident Response Criteria

- documented process: an approved incident-response policy, plan, procedure, or playbook describes detection, triage, containment, eradication, recovery, and/or communication.
- assigned roles: named roles or teams have incident-response responsibilities.
- reporting channel: employees or stakeholders have a defined way to report incidents.
- escalation procedure: severe incidents can be escalated to defined roles, leadership, legal, privacy, or crisis management functions.
- supplier escalation: supplier or third-party incident handling and notification paths are described.
- periodic testing/tabletop exercise: evidence shows testing, drills, tabletop exercises, or exercise planning.
- post-incident review: lessons learned, corrective actions, or post-incident review are described.
- evidence preservation: incident records, logs, forensic evidence, or chain-of-custody expectations are described.
- management approval/owner: document owner or management approval is clear.
- version/review validity: document version, date, review cycle, or validity status is clear.

## Status Labels

- fulfilled: all criteria required by the case are sufficiently supported by implementation-capable organizational evidence.
- partially_fulfilled: at least one required criterion is supported, but one or more required criteria are missing, outdated, ambiguous, or insufficient.
- not_fulfilled: the core requirement is absent, contradicted, or supported only by non-implementation evidence.
- unclear: evidence is relevant but not enough to decide confidently, or source/metadata quality prevents a stronger status.

## Non-Implementation Evidence

Templates, public guidance, NIST/OSCAL/FedRAMP/BSI control text, blank assessment templates, and generic public guidance should not count as organizational implementation evidence unless the requirement explicitly asks for guidance-level context.
